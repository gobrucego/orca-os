12 Week Cycle

Collagen turnover takes time—about 12 weeks for new fibers to mature and integrate into your skin's structure. The three phases work sequentially: activation primes your cells, remodeling builds new structure, and maintenance preserves the improvement.

Maintenance

After 12 weeks, take 4-6 weeks off to allow receptor reset. This prevents tolerance and keeps your cells responsive. Then you can either repeat the full cycle or use quarterly 4-week boosters to maintain results. Most people run 2-3 full cycles per year (spring and fall are popular) with boosters in between.

SUPPORTING THE PROCESS

Co-factors for collagen formation

  • Vitamin C 250–500 mg/day — cofactor for pro-collagen hydroxylation
  • Zinc 10–15 mg/day — maintains Cu/Zn balance and supports matrix enzymes
  • Hydration: 2–3 L/day, omega-3–rich diet, minimal alcohol

Barrier care

  • Use ceramide or lipid-based moisturizers nightly
  • Topical GHK-Cu (0.5–1%) post-LED or microneedling enhances uptake and surface tone
  • Avoid combining topical copper peptides with strong vitamin C or acid serums—oxidative interference can deactivate GHK-Cu
FOR WOMEN OVER 40

Estrogen decline after age 40 reduces collagen density by approximately 1% per year. Women in peri- or post-menopause may benefit from quarterly GLOW or KLOW cycles (3–4× yearly) to maintain structural integrity.

Unlike fillers (which add volume), retinoids (which force turnover), or Botox (which paralyzes muscles), peptides work with your body's existing repair systems. The result is regeneration, not replacement—skin that functions better, not just looks temporarily improved.
